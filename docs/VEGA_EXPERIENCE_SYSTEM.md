# Vega Experience System

Vega should feel like a sales director running a coordinated acquisition operation, not a static lead list.

## Customer Experience

- Start with a website, a business description, or both.
- Show discovered business facts for confirmation before treating them as true.
- Recommend target markets, buyer roles, local/referral categories, intent signals, channel policy, fulfillment mode, and product tier.
- Keep setup short and conversational.

## Operator Experience

- Show the next move, the reason, and the evidence.
- Separate automatic email, approval email, call-first, research, suppression, and closed lanes.
- Treat phone assist as a human conversion queue with assignment, due time, attempts, outcome, callback, and meeting handoff.
- Let Slack commands control Vega without relying on web redirects.

## Proof Experience

Reports must distinguish activity from conversion:

- Sent is not delivered.
- Open/click is not a reply.
- Phone task is not a call.
- Voicemail is not reached contact.
- Meeting request is not booked.
- Booked is not attended.
- Proposal is not revenue.

## Phase A Status

The Phase A foundation adds intent scoring, next-best-channel decisions, source quality, provider adapters, entitlements, feature flags, and controlled experiment records. Phase B/C interfaces should use this foundation instead of hard-coding rules in UI components.
